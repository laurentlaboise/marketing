3. Categories of Personal Information We Collect

3.1 Information you provide

  • Identity and contact data: name, email address, phone number (including WhatsApp), company name, job title, country/region.
  • Account and profile data: login identifiers, preferences, role, and authentication metadata.
  • Commercial and billing data: package selected, invoices, status of remittances, billing address, and limited commercial metadata from processors (we do not store full card primary account numbers on our servers when using PCI-compliant gateways).
  • Customer Content: briefs, brand assets, documents, copy, prompts, form submissions, support messages, and other materials you upload or send.
  • Marketing preferences: newsletter opt-in/opt-out and communication choices.

3.2 Information collected automatically

  • Device and log data: IP address, browser type, device identifiers, operating system, referring URLs, pages viewed, timestamps, and approximate location derived from IP.
  • Cookies and similar technologies: as described in our Cookie Policy and Consent Management Platform (CMP).
  • Analytics events: usage and performance metrics (for example Google Analytics property identifiers such as G-LMRKC1VBBB when enabled under your consent choices).

3.3 Information from third parties

We may receive information from commercial processors, advertising platforms, social networks (if you interact with our pages), analytics providers, and partners who refer you to us, consistent with their policies and applicable law.

4. Purposes of Collection, Use, and Disclosure (Meaningful Consent)

We collect, use, and disclose personal information for the following purposes, for which we seek meaningful consent or rely on another lawful basis where permitted:

  • provide, operate, secure, and improve the Services;
  • create and manage accounts; authenticate users;
  • process transactions, subscriptions, refunds, and tax documentation;
  • deliver digital marketing, web, content, SEO, automation, and AI-assisted services you request;
  • communicate about orders, support, security alerts, and service changes;
  • send marketing communications where you have consented or where soft opt-in is permitted by law (you may withdraw consent at any time);
  • measure performance, debug errors, and analyze aggregated trends;
  • prevent fraud, abuse, and security incidents; enforce Terms;
  • comply with legal obligations and respond to lawful requests;
  • establish, exercise, or defend legal claims.

We do not use personal information for purposes that a reasonable person would not consider appropriate in the circumstances without additional notice and, where required, consent.

5. AI Processing, LLMs, and Automation Pipelines

How AI is used. Portions of the Services process Customer Content and user inputs through automated workflows and third-party large language models (LLMs) (for example OpenAI and other model providers we configure) to generate drafts, analyze content, route tasks, or produce deliverables.

  • Third-party LLM processing: User inputs, documents, and prompts you submit to AI-enabled features may be transmitted to third-party processors solely to generate the requested output and to operate the feature. Those processors process data under their terms and data-processing arrangements with us.
  • Ephemeral vs stored: Some AI request/response pairs are processed transiently to produce an output and are not retained by us beyond operational logs and backup cycles. Other content (for example project files, chat history in a dashboard, form submissions, or approved deliverables) may be stored for the duration of your engagement and for the retention periods in Section 10.
  • Model training: We do not use your Customer Content to train our own proprietary foundation models. We do not authorize third-party LLM providers to use your Customer Content to train their public models where the provider offers an enterprise or API setting that disables training; where a provider’s default consumer product would allow training, we configure available controls to opt out. Residual risk remains that a subprocessor’s systems process data according to their then-current policies—we will update this Policy if that material fact changes.
  • Human review: Staff or contractors (including personnel operating from Lao PDR) may review inputs and outputs to deliver quality control, support, and fraud prevention.
  • Your responsibility: Do not submit special-category data, health data, children’s data, government identifiers, or card primary account numbers via free-text AI fields unless we have a written processing agreement covering that data.

7. Cross-Border Transfers and Data Sovereignty

Personal information is primarily handled in connection with operations in Lao PDR and may be processed, stored, and accessed in other countries, including:

  • Lao PDR — primary operational management, support, and delivery staff;
  • United States — many cloud, AI, commercial, and analytics providers;
  • Other countries — where subprocessors maintain infrastructure or support centers (which may include Canada or elsewhere after any future entity shift).

When personal information is transferred outside Lao PDR, it may be subject to the laws of those jurisdictions, including lawful access by foreign authorities. We use contractual and organizational measures appropriate to the risk (including vendor diligence and data-processing terms where available). By using the Services or submitting personal information, you acknowledge these cross-border transfers.

For GDPR, where we act as controller for EU/UK data subjects, our legal bases typically include: contract performance; legitimate interests (security, improvement, B2B outreach balanced against your rights); consent (cookies/marketing/AI free-text where required); and legal obligation. International transfers rely on appropriate safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses where required.

8. Cookies and Tracking

We use essential, analytics, and marketing cookies as classified in our Cookie Policy. Non-essential cookies require active opt-in via our Consent Management Platform (CMP) before those scripts load, particularly for European and Californian traffic. 9. Security

We implement administrative, technical, and organizational measures designed to protect personal information against loss, theft, unauthorized access, disclosure, copying, use, or modification. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure; you use the Services at your own residual risk. Report suspected incidents promptly to privacy@wordsthatsells.website.

10. Retention

We retain personal information only as long as necessary for the purposes identified, including:

  • active account and project data — for the life of the relationship;
  • billing and tax records — typically seven (7) years or as required by law;
  • marketing contacts — until you withdraw consent or after a period of inactivity consistent with our practices;
  • security logs — for a limited period for investigation and integrity;
  • backups — until rotated out of backup cycles.

When retention ends, we delete or anonymize data, subject to legal holds.

11. Your Rights (Lao PDR, GDPR, CCPA, and PIPEDA-aligned baselines)

Subject to legal exceptions, you may request:

  • Access to personal information we hold about you;
  • Correction of inaccurate or incomplete information;
  • Deletion / erasure where applicable;
  • Withdrawal of consent for processing based on consent (without affecting prior lawful processing);
  • Portability of certain data (GDPR, where applicable);
  • Restriction or objection to certain processing (GDPR, where applicable);
  • Opt-out of sale/share and certain targeted advertising (CCPA/CPRA, where applicable);
  • Appeal of a denial of a consumer request where required by law.

How to exercise rights: Email privacy@wordsthatsells.website with the subject line “Privacy Request” and specify the right you wish to exercise. We may need to verify your identity. We will respond within the timelines required by applicable law (we aim to respond within thirty (30) days, subject to permitted extensions under applicable law; for GDPR, generally within one month).

You may also lodge a complaint with the competent authority in Lao PDR, with the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada if Canadian law later applies to you after an entity shift, or with your local data protection authority if GDPR applies. We encourage you to contact us first so we can address your concern.
