1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. Similar technologies include local storage, session storage, pixels, tags, and software development kits (SDKs). Together they help sites function, measure performance, and (with consent) support marketing.

2. Strict Classification of Trackers

2.1 Essential cookies (always on)

These are required for security, network management, load balancing, consent storage, login/session integrity, fraud prevention, and core site functionality. They cannot be disabled through our CMP without impairing the Services. Examples: session identifiers, CSRF tokens, consent preference records, load-balancer stickiness.

2.2 Analytics cookies (opt-in)

These measure traffic, performance, and feature usage so we can improve the Services. Examples: Google Analytics (property G-LMRKC1VBBB when enabled), aggregated page-view and engagement metrics. Analytics cookies load only after you actively opt in via the Consent Management Platform (CMP).

2.3 Marketing cookies (opt-in)

These support advertising measurement, retargeting, and campaign attribution across sites or platforms (for example Meta, LinkedIn, Google Ads pixels when deployed). Marketing cookies load only after you actively opt in via the CMP. Consent is not implied by mere use of the site.

3. Consent Architecture (CMP)

We operate a Consent Management Platform (CMP) in tandem with this Policy:

  • No assumed consent for non-essential cookies. Simply browsing does not constitute consent for Analytics or Marketing trackers.
  • Active opt-in is required before Analytics or Marketing scripts are loaded, particularly for users in the European Economic Area, United Kingdom, and California, and as a fairness standard for other visitors.
  • Granular choice: You may accept all, reject non-essential, or customize categories (Essential / Analytics / Marketing).
  • Withdrawal: You may change or withdraw consent at any time through the CMP interface (cookie settings link or banner re-open control). Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.
  • Proof of consent: We store a record of your CMP choices (typically as an Essential cookie or equivalent) so we can honor and demonstrate your preferences.

If a CMP banner is not yet available on a particular page, non-essential tags must remain blocked until consent tooling is active; contact privacy@wordsthatsells.website if you believe non-essential cookies fired without consent.

4. Session vs Persistent Cookies — Expiration

  • Session cookies expire when you close your browser (or shortly thereafter). They typically support login state and temporary navigation.
  • Persistent cookies remain until they reach their set expiry or you delete them. Typical ranges:
    • consent preference cookies: up to twelve (12) months;
    • analytics cookies: from twenty-four (24) hours up to twenty-four (24) months depending on provider settings;
    • marketing/retargeting cookies: commonly from thirty (30) days up to twelve (12) months, subject to platform defaults and your browser settings.

Exact lifetimes may vary by vendor. You can delete cookies through your browser settings at any time; doing so may reset consent state and require new choices on the next visit.
