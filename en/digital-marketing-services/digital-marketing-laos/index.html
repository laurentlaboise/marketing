Digital Marketing in Laos
Practical growth for Lao SMEs — Facebook first, Google Maps done right

Your customers are already online. The question is whether they find you — or your competitor.

If you run a shop, clinic, restaurant, or service business in Laos, you already know where your customers spend their time: Facebook. They message a page before they call. They check your photos before they visit. And when they need directions or opening hours, they search Google Maps.

The problem is not that digital marketing doesn't work in Laos. The problem is that most SME owners have no time to do it. Posts stop after two weeks. Messages sit unanswered. The Google listing shows the wrong hours — or doesn't exist at all. Meanwhile, the business down the street that posts consistently keeps winning the customers.

That is the gap we close. WordsThatSells is headquartered in Vientiane, and we run digital marketing for Lao SMEs on clearly scoped monthly plans — you always know what is included, what it costs, and what happens next month.

Why Facebook-first works in Laos

In many markets, agencies push websites and complex funnels first. In Laos, that order is backwards. Here, Facebook is where discovery, conversation, and buying decisions actually happen — so that is where your marketing should start.

Customers message first

Lao buyers ask questions in Messenger before they spend. A page that posts regularly and answers quickly looks open for business. A silent page looks closed — even if your shop is full.

Your page is your storefront

For most Lao SMEs, the Facebook page does the job a website does elsewhere: photos, prices, promotions, reviews, and directions. It deserves the same care you give your physical shopfront.

Consistency beats bursts

One month of daily posts followed by silence teaches the algorithm — and your followers — to forget you. A steady cadence of 8 well-made posts per month outperforms unplanned bursts.

Google Business Profile done right in Vientiane

Facebook wins attention, but Google Maps wins intent. When someone in Vientiane searches "coffee near me", "dentist Vientiane", or your business name, Google shows a map with a short list of local businesses at the top. If your Google Business Profile is missing, incomplete, or showing last year's opening hours, that customer goes to whoever is listed properly.

Doing it "right" is specific work: claiming and verifying the listing, choosing the correct categories, keeping hours and phone numbers current, adding photos that show the real business, and answering the questions and reviews that appear. It is not glamorous — and that is why most Lao businesses never finish it. The upside for those who do is a free spot on the map where buying decisions happen. Our guide to Google Maps SEO explains the mechanics.

Every plan we run for Lao SMEs treats the Google Business Profile as core infrastructure, not an afterthought. Facebook brings the conversation; Google Maps brings the customer who is already looking for you. Together they cover both halves of how people buy locally: the ones scrolling past your business, and the ones actively searching for it.
